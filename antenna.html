Antenna Tools

Antennas, ranges and practical test geometry.

A short introduction to the antenna work that sits alongside the EMC section, plus practical tools for range regions and beamwidth data.

My first experience with antennas goes back to my apprenticeship with QinetiQ at Aberporth in West Wales, where antennas were part of the everyday test environment rather than an abstract classroom topic.

I then worked with antennas in a smaller but still practical way through Powerlink and the amplifier repair centre in Hook, Hampshire, where RF hardware, amplifier behaviour and test equipment were much closer to the bench.

At Airbus I saw my first planar range and began the path that pulled antenna measurement, EMC and spacecraft qualification together. That experience became more direct again at Mangata, where I worked closely with suppliers to investigate how a near-field range could be installed in Scotland to test the satellites we intended to build.

The tools below reflect that practical thread: understanding where an antenna is being used, what region a test distance sits in, and how beamwidth information can be captured from manufacturer plots for real test planning.
