Shaker sizing

Before a vibration test is booked, someone has to check the shaker can actually do it. Force equals the total moving mass times the acceleration — and the total includes everything bolted to the armature, not just the unit under test.

F  =  (mEUT + mfixture + marmature) · a · kmargin

Inputs

Head expanders count too. Fixtures are often heavier than the EUT.
From the shaker datasheet — the moving element itself.
Peak sine level, or ~3× gRMS for random as a working rule.
Headroom for control overshoot, notching studies and reality.

Shaker ratings differ between sine, random and shock — check the required force against the correct column of the datasheet. For random tests the rating is usually quoted in force RMS; the 3× rule of thumb here is planning guidance, not a substitute for the control-system prediction.
