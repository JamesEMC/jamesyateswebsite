MIL-STD-461 FFT receiver four-panel reproduction.

This tool explains the MIL-STD-461G Appendix A receiver example behind Figures A-5 to A-8. A traditional receiver sweeps through the band and may catch repeated pulses at several tuned frequencies, while an FFT receiver samples a wider time block and converts it into the frequency domain. That can be much faster and can capture frequency-agile emissions well, but a short FFT dwell can miss a low-repetition-rate pulse completely. The default example uses a 1 Hz, 10 µs, 0.1 V pulse train to show why a 15 ms FFT dwell may miss the event, while a 1 s dwell captures the pulse period and reveals the spectral envelope.

Reference

This page is based on MIL-STD-461G Appendix A, section A.4.3.10.3.3 on frequency scanning, Table II measurement timing, and Figures A-5 through A-8. The standard explains that FFT receivers can reduce measurement time and capture frequency-agile signals, but low-repetition-rate signals can be missed when FFT dwell time is shorter than the pulse repetition interval.

Amplitude and attenuation factor

Pulse amplitude defaults to 0.1 V peak. The plotted level shifts by 20 log10(V/0.1). The Attenuation Factor (dB) is then added to the displayed level. The default is -20 dB, which visually aligns this simplified model with the duplicated MIL plots. The traditional receiver traces are generated from an actual sweep rate across the selected span, so their spacing follows the scan time shown in each plot title and the pulse repetition rate. Sinc-null markers are calculated from the current pulse width, not hard-coded. The MIL helper also updates when you change start/stop frequency, so it can show conducted or radiated-style bandwidth regions. The CE102 overlay now uses the interpreted 115 V limit line: 100 dBµV at 10 kHz, rolling down on a log-frequency basis to 66 dBµV at 500 kHz, then flat to 10 MHz. The noise/seed/download controls live behind the ☰ menu. The CE102 115 V reference line is always shown. The x-axis can be switched between linear and logarithmic scaling, and the tick spacing is automatically limited to about 10 labels.

What is intentionally simplified

This is a behavioural teaching model, not a certified EMI receiver. It does not replace the exact standard, test procedure, receiver architecture, preselector behaviour, detector dynamics, correction factors or facility software validation.

Random seed

The default seed is 461. In Fixed seed mode, repeated runs are identical. In Random each run mode, pressing the reproduce button generates a new seed and therefore a new timing phase between the pulse train and receiver start time. This shifts the regular traditional-receiver spike pattern and can change whether the short FFT dwell captures or misses the pulse. The seed also controls the noise-floor realization.
