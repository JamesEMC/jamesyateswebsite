Where it comes from

The road through EMC.

Airbus came first: table-top EMC testing of satellite and ground station equipment to MIL-STD-based internal plans. Then eleven years at Element, where I learned commercial EMC test work and the complete range of tests around it, at high throughput — the environment where you learn what standards really mean by getting them wrong under supervision, and climb from technician to laboratory manager while you do. Back to Airbus with a more holistic approach, testing and analysing spacecraft and payload hardware to MIL-STD-461 and ESA requirements. Then facility definition at Mangata as Director of Spacecraft Test, aerospace and defence business management at Eurofins, and now Lead EMC Engineer at Leonardo, supporting radar and combat air programmes.

Every tool in this section exists because a real job needed it — to make a calculation faster, a setup decision clearer, or a teaching point stick.
