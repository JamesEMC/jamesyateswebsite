Where these rules come from

RE103 measures an antenna-connected transmitter's spurious emissions, so the measurement antenna must sit in the transmit antenna's far field. MIL-STD-461G gives the distance rules this page applies: at or below 1.24 GHz, the greater of 2D²/λ and 3λ; above 1.24 GHz, 2D²/λ where the receive aperture is small compared with the source (2.5D < d fails), otherwise (D+d)²/λ.

RS103 radiates a field at the EUT, and the standard requires the antenna at least 1 m from the setup boundary, with enough antenna positions that no point of the boundary is more than 1.5 m from an antenna centreline — in practice, one position per 3 m of boundary. The coverage figure here uses the antenna's 3 dB beamwidth to show how wide a strip your antenna genuinely illuminates at the proposed distance, which is the practical constraint people forget.

DO-160G Section 20 similarly wants far-field conditions for its radiated susceptibility work; the classic 2D²/λ figure is shown against your proposed distance.

As always: these checks encode the headline geometry rules to speed up planning. The standard you're testing to — and your lab's accredited procedure — take precedence over anything on this page.
